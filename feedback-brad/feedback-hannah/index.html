How the process went

The search

This was a competitive process. A talent pool of 163 candidates was identified, over 35 discovery conversations were held, 20 first interviews conducted and 9 competency interviews completed. Five candidates were recommended for final interview. You were one of them — and with very good reason.

Your interviews

Your first interview was one of the most natural and engaging in the process. Your community fundraising instinct — the nursing staff engagement in Kerikeri, the ward visits at Rowcroft, the home visits at ECOE — was the most authentic and practically grounded of any candidate. Your cause connection was specific, personal and genuinely felt. Your breadth across 20 years of building fundraising from scratch in different contexts and different countries made for a compelling picture of someone who can do everything a small charity needs.

Feedback from the client

What Thrive said about you

Matthew and Val were clear: you were appointable. In Matthew's words, if the appointed candidate had not been there, the decision between you and another finalist would have been extraordinarily difficult — both of you were genuinely in contention.

What came through most powerfully for the panel was your values, your personal connections, and your real understanding of what makes an individual donor tick from a relationship perspective. Your instinct for the human side of fundraising — the story, the connection, the relationship — was amongst the strongest in the room.

The margin that made the difference was on the process and systems side — the data management, stewardship journey design, segmentation and tracking elements that sit alongside the relational work. The panel described it as a juxtaposition: you were strong on the relational dimension, another finalist was strong on the process dimension, and the appointed candidate demonstrated both in equal measure.

This was a very fine distinction. You were appointable. You were in contention. The panel would have had a very difficult decision if the field had been different.

Looking ahead

A development observation — not a gap

The feedback is not that you lack process capability — your 20 years of building fundraising infrastructure from scratch demonstrates clearly that you have it. What the feedback reflects is that in the interview setting, the relational and values dimensions came through more prominently than the systems and data dimensions.

In a future interview for a role with a significant individual giving and stewardship component, it is worth being ready to talk as fluently about segmentation, stewardship journeys and data-led decision making as you do about relationships and cause connection. The substance is there in your career. It is about making sure it surfaces clearly alongside everything else.

Moving forward

You should be genuinely proud. You were in a strong field and you were absolutely in contention for a role that attracted some exceptional candidates. That is a real and meaningful result.
