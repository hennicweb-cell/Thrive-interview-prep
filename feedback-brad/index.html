How the process went

The search

This was a competitive process. A talent pool of 163 candidates was identified, over 35 discovery conversations were held, 20 first interviews conducted and 9 competency interviews completed. Five candidates were recommended for final interview. You were one of them — and with good reason.

Your interviews

Your competency interview was the most consistent and reliable performance in the field. You answered every question directly and clearly without prompting across 40 minutes. Your acquisition thinking was the most commercially structured of any candidate — the cold mailing and press insert proposal, correctly framed as volume before income with ROI expectations set realistically, was exactly the kind of answer the role required. Your management evidence was the strongest in the process. The story you shared about an ABF beneficiary whose experience genuinely changed how you worked was the most compelling cause-connection answer of the entire search.

Feedback from the client

What Thrive said about you

Matthew and Val were clear: you were appointable. There was never any question about your capability or the quality of your interview. You came across really well, answered the questions really well, and were genuinely strong throughout.

The decision came down to a very fine balance between two qualities that the very best fundraisers hold simultaneously — the process and systems side, and the relational side. Your natural strength, which came through clearly in your interview, was on the process, data, journey design, testing and stewardship side. You demonstrated real commercial rigour and a clear methodology.

The appointed candidate demonstrated both of those things in equal measure — marginally stronger on the relational dimension in the room. Matthew was direct about this: if you had pitched up just a little more relational in how you presented yourself, you might have given them a tighter run. That is an incredibly fine margin.

Matthew also said explicitly: if the appointed candidate had not been in the process, the decision would have been extraordinarily difficult. You were absolutely in contention.

Looking ahead

A development observation — not a gap

The feedback is not that you lack relational capability — everything about how you work suggests you have it in abundance. The story you told about your ABF beneficiary proved that. What the feedback suggests is that in a formal interview setting, your natural instinct is to lead with the process and mechanics — which is your commercial strength — and the relational dimension, while present, came through less prominently.

In a future interview for a community-facing role, it is worth being conscious of leading with the human story first, and the process that followed from it second. The substance is there. It is about the order in which you surface it.

Moving forward

You should feel genuinely proud of how you performed in this process. You were in a strong field and you were absolutely in contention for a role that was keenly contested. That is a real and meaningful result.

Please do stay in touch. I would very much like to work with you again and I will be in touch if I have something that feels right.
