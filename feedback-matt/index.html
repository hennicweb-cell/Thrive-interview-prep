How the process went

The search

This was a competitive process. A talent pool of 163 candidates was identified, over 35 discovery conversations were held, 20 first interviews conducted and 9 competency interviews completed. You were recommended for final client interview — a reflection of the genuine strength of your individual giving track record and your personal connection to the role.

Your experience and application

Your RUHX build story is genuinely exceptional. Taking a programme from near-zero to over £500,000 across multiple streams — door drops, in-memory giving, a staff lottery, digital advertising, and a personally built network of funeral director relationships — without significant team or agency support is a real and rare achievement. Your design capability, your personal connection to Somerset, and your cover letter were all genuinely strong elements of your application.

Feedback from the client

What Thrive said about you

Matthew and Val were positive about your individual giving capability and your stewardship instincts — both came through clearly. They could see the quality of what you have built at RUHX and it impressed them.

The honest feedback is that the interview itself did not fully do justice to the strength of your experience. The panel felt that your examples and experience were not always translated into sufficiently clear and structured answers for them to feel fully confident across the board. The vision, the personal connection, and the strength of your RUHX story did not come through as powerfully in the formal interview setting as they had the potential to.

The areas where others pulled ahead were community fundraising and the breadth of personal connection demonstrated in the room on the day. Your individual giving and stewardship were clear strengths — but in a role where community culture-building alongside 70 village agents is central, the panel needed to hear more about how you would approach that dimension specifically.

Looking ahead

A development observation

The feedback points to one clear area for development: translating your experience into structured, specific, well-evidenced answers under formal interview conditions. The capability and the experience are genuinely there — the RUHX story alone is one of the strongest individual giving build stories I have encountered in this sector. The gap is in making sure that comes through as clearly in the interview room as it does in conversation.

For future senior interviews, it is worth investing time in preparing structured examples — the situation, what you did, and specifically what the outcome was — for the dimensions that matter most to the role. Practising those answers out loud will make a significant difference.

Your personal connection to Somerset and to the communities you want to serve is genuine and compelling. Make sure that comes through early and clearly in the room — it is one of your strongest assets.

Moving forward

Please do not be disheartened. You are a genuinely strong candidate with a track record that is directly relevant to the roles you are going after. The experience is real and impressive. This was a strong field and the margins were fine.

I will very much stay in touch and I look forward to working with you again. The right role is coming.
