About Thrive

Who they are

Thrive is the operating name of the Community Council for Somerset — a charity with over 100 years of history (2026 is their centenary year). Registered at Fitzwarren House, Queen Street, Taunton. Charity number 1069260.

They have historically operated through contracts with Somerset County Council and are now actively repositioning — moving from contract delivery toward a more mission-focused model with growing unrestricted fundraising income. This is a genuine and significant pivot.

Their belief: everyone in Somerset should be able to live a healthy, independent and safe life and be part of a thriving community.

What they do — services and the village agent model

Thrive's work covers three areas: individuals and families (debt advice, fuel poverty support, mental health signposting); community buildings and places (training for people running local community spaces); and diverse and displaced communities (support for minority ethnic communities and refugees).

Their 70 village agents are paid staff — not volunteers — embedded across Somerset. They visit people at home, run talking cafes, provide advice and crisis support, and connect people to services. The FM's job is strategic enablement — building fundraising culture with the agents, designing toolkits, creating data capture systems — not operational delivery at every event.

The fundraising picture

Thrive's income is almost entirely statutory contracts and grants. A very small number of existing regular givers. The real gap is donor acquisition. The centenary goal is 2,000 actively interested supporters by year end — not donors yet. 100 regular givers by year end would be welcome but is not a hard target.

There is no fixed acquisition budget. Every spend must be proposed with a clear rationale: channel, likely response, cost per supporter, break-even at regular giving level. Email lists exist (a few thousand contacts) but are not consented for fundraising — Thrive must not fundraise from current clients. Beacon CRM is in place.

Culture, values and the growth trajectory

Thrive genuinely lives its values: kindness and compassion, honesty and transparency, quality and diversity. Family-friendly, flexible, and an environment where it is okay to take risks and fail. Increasingly mission-focused — frontline staff will notice immediately whether a fundraiser genuinely cares.

If the FM performs well in year one, a digital fundraising officer follows in year two and the FM grows into head of fundraising. This is a builder role with a genuine career trajectory.

Areas to Prepare

Donor acquisition — prepare a structured answer

Your instinct is warm-audience and events-first, which is consistent and logical. The gap is commercial framing — cost, projected response rate, break-even at regular giving level. Matthew needs to hear that you can construct a proposal, not just a plan.

Before the interview, prepare one clear acquisition answer. Draw on what you know from CHAT and what you understand about Thrive's centenary events: 'My first proposal would be to use the centenary events as paid acquisition opportunities — marketing them to the wider community through print in GP surgeries, community noticeboards and village halls, plus a small targeted social media campaign. Budget of approximately £X for a defined test. At a 2% sign-up rate, with post-event personal conversations and a newsletter journey, I would project Y new consented contacts. At £10/month regular giving, break-even is around Z months. I would report back after the first three events before proposing any wider rollout.' You do not need exact numbers. You need the structure.

Year one — be specific about what you would do first

Matthew wants someone who can walk in and describe exactly what the first six months looks like. Prepare a clear answer: what you would do in month one, what the first three months look like, and what you would want to have achieved by month six. Lead with the practical — Beacon audit, village agent meetings, grant pipeline review, centenary events data capture — before moving to strategy.

The Creative Planner Co — address it yourself

Your parallel business is something Matthew may ask about. Get ahead of it rather than waiting. Something like: 'I want to be straightforward about my creative business — it runs in evenings and weekends and has done since 2019. It does not compete for my professional headspace and it will not affect my commitment here. If anything, the event management, marketing and audience engagement skills it has given me are directly relevant.' Said confidently and early, it becomes a positive rather than a concern.
